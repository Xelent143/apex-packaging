## LinkedIn Post

Cosmetic packaging quotes usually break down after the first visual approval, not before it.

That happens when the buyer sends dimensions but leaves out the finish risk, insert needs, retail route, or sample plan.

For a launch or multi-SKU cosmetic program, a stronger brief usually answers:

1. What product is going into the carton, and what are the exact dimensions and weight?
2. Does the pack need a folding carton, rigid setup, shipper, mailer, or a combination of those formats?
3. Which surfaces are likely to scuff, dent, rub, or shift during packing and shipment?
4. Is an insert required to control fit, presentation, or breakage during handling?
5. Is artwork approved, or does the team still need dieline and print-prep support?
6. Does the launch need samples to confirm fit, closure, finish, and shelf presentation before production?

That matters because a lower first quote does not help if the finish changes, the insert gets added later, or the transit route forces a structural revision after approval.
