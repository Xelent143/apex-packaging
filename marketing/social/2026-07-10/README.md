## LinkedIn Post

Poly tubing and poly sheeting are usually better buying decisions when the line needs flexibility, not just film.

That matters in a few common situations:

1. Product lengths vary and a fixed finished bag creates waste
2. Oversized items or pallet loads need coverage that is awkward in a stock bag
3. The plant wants fewer packaging SKUs in inventory
4. The line already cuts and seals film at the point of use

This is where procurement teams can improve the quote quality quickly.

Instead of asking for "clear poly," send the format and line details first:

- tubing, flat sheeting, center-fold, or finished bag
- layflat width and cut length range
- gauge target or performance goal
- seal method
- vent or perforation needs
- manual or automatic loading method
- food-contact, recycled-content, or pallet-handling requirements

The latest Apex guide breaks down when roll stock is more practical than pre-made bags and which specifications matter before pricing.

## Facebook / Instagram Caption

Not every poly film request should start with a finished bag.

If your line packs variable lengths, oversized items, pallet loads, or mixed SKUs, poly tubing or poly sheeting may be the cleaner option.

Why?

- the line can cut the exact length it needs
- one roll program can reduce finished-bag SKUs
- pallet covers, liners, and dust protection often need flat sheeting instead of a bag
- the quote gets more accurate when sealing and handling are defined early

Before sending an RFQ, include:

- film format
- layflat width
- cut length range
- gauge target
- seal method
- vent or perforation needs
- loading style
- delivery location and volume
