## LinkedIn Post

Soap packaging quotes usually get more expensive after the first visual approval, not before it.

That happens when the buyer sends dimensions but leaves out the route, the board preference, the moisture risk, or the sample plan.

For handmade and retail soap programs, a stronger brief usually answers:

1. What are the exact product dimensions and weight?
2. Does the product need a kraft carton, a sleeve, a window box, a mailer, or a combined retail-and-shipping format?
3. Will the package sit on a shelf, ship parcel, go into a subscription box, or move through a mixed route?
4. Is the carton likely to face humidity, scuffing, oil transfer, or close contact with the product wrap?
5. Is artwork approved, or does the team still need dieline and print-prep support?
6. Does the order need a sample to confirm fit, closure, shelf look, and packout before production?

That matters because a lower first quote does not help if the board changes, the window patch gets added later, or the moisture handling requirement shows up after approval.
