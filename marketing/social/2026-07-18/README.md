## LinkedIn Post

Protective packaging quotes usually break down when the supplier has to guess how the product actually moves.

For fragile product kits, a stronger brief usually answers:

1. What are the product dimensions, packed weight, and fragile surfaces?
2. Does the shipment move parcel, courier, LTL, pallet, or mixed distribution?
3. Is the protection coming from foam, corrugated inserts, void fill, molded pulp, or a combination?
4. Does the team need a sample to confirm fit, closure, and packing speed?
5. Are there artwork, sustainability, or reorder-control requirements that should be priced from the start?

That matters because a protective-packaging program is not just an outer carton.

The internal fit changes damage risk.
The route changes the board and cushioning choice.
The sample process changes how confidently the team can approve production.
