## LinkedIn Post

Wicket bags look simple until the line starts fighting the spec.

Produce and bakery teams usually feel the issue in one of four places:

1. The film is chosen on habit instead of product weight and handling
2. The vent pattern is copied from another SKU without reviewing strength loss
3. Wicket spacing or lip length does not match the real loading setup
4. The bag is quoted separately from the corrugated shipper and case count

That is why the best wicket bag RFQ is operational, not generic.

It should name the bag width and length, film preference or performance goal, vent pattern, packed product weight, wicket details, stack count, closure method, and units per case.

The latest Apex guide breaks down how produce and bakery buyers can quote wicket bags around line speed, airflow, presentation, and outer-case fit instead of treating them like a commodity bag.
