## LinkedIn Post

Protective packaging inserts should be chosen around the way the product fails, not around whichever material the team used last time.

That usually means separating four questions:

1. Is the product breaking from impact, scuffing on contact, or shifting inside the shipper?
2. Does the route need cushioning, blocking and bracing, or a cleaner presentation fit?
3. Can the outer carton change, or does the insert need to solve the problem inside the current box?
4. Is the sustainability goal paper-based material, damage reduction, or both?

That is why "we need foam" is often too early as a spec.

A stronger RFQ names:

- product dimensions and weight
- surface sensitivity or break points
- current damage history
- shipping route and handling method
- outer carton size and board grade
- preferred insert direction: foam, molded pulp, corrugated, or recommendation

## Facebook / Instagram Caption

Not every product-protection problem needs the same insert material.

Foam, molded pulp, and corrugated do different jobs:

- foam for higher cushioning and delicate surfaces
- molded pulp for repeatable shapes and paper-based programs
- corrugated for blocking, separation, and structural support

The better quote starts with the actual failure mode:

- what breaks or scuffs
- how it ships
- what the outer carton is doing now
- what sustainability target still has to be met

If the team sends product dimensions, weight, route, damage history, and outer-box details first, the insert recommendation gets much cleaner.
