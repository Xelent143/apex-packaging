## LinkedIn Post

Corrugated shipping box decisions usually get more expensive when buyers compare price before they compare route conditions.

For heavy ecommerce and parcel programs, a stronger brief usually answers:

1. What is the packed weight, not just the bare product weight?
2. Is the route parcel, LTL, palletized storage, or a mix?
3. Does the carton need ECT-32, ECT-44, or a double-wall review?
4. Are inserts, pads, or edge protection part of the current failure pattern?
5. Is the job being quoted against the real pallet pattern and reorder cadence?

That matters because a corrugated shipping box is not one generic line item.

Board grade changes compression performance.
Flute choice changes structure and print surface.
Pallet handling changes whether the same box survives the full route.
