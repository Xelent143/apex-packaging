## LinkedIn Post

Poly tubing and poly sheeting are often the better choice when the pack line needs flexibility, not just film.

That usually shows up in a few cases:

1. Product lengths vary and a fixed finished bag creates waste
2. Oversized items need custom-cut coverage
3. The plant wants fewer packaging SKUs in inventory
4. The line already cuts and seals film at the point of use

The common mistake is asking for "clear poly" before defining how the film will actually work.

A stronger RFQ starts with:

- tubing, flat sheeting, center-fold, or finished bag
- layflat width and cut length range
- gauge target or performance requirement
- seal method and loading style
- vent, perforation, food-contact, or PCR-content needs
- delivery location, volume, and release schedule

The newest Apex guide explains when roll stock beats a pre-made bag and which details help suppliers quote the right format faster.
