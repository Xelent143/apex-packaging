## LinkedIn Post

Tea packaging quotes usually go sideways after the first concept review, not before it.

That happens when the buyer sends dimensions but leaves out the actual format, the route, the aroma-protection requirement, or the sample plan.

For tea packaging boxes, a stronger brief usually answers:

1. Is the product shipping in a folding carton, tin sleeve, stand-up pouch, gift set, or a mixed format?
2. What are the exact product dimensions, fill weight, and case-pack needs?
3. Will the package sit on a retail shelf, ship parcel, move in multipacks, or go through a mixed route?
4. Does the product need aroma protection, a tighter closure, or an insert for presentation and fit?
5. Is artwork approved, or does the team still need dieline and print-prep support?
6. Does the launch need samples to confirm fit, closure, shelf look, and packout before production?
7. Are there sustainability or recycled-content requirements that need documentation?

That matters because a lower first quote does not help if the format changes later, the insert is added after approval, or the route forces a structural revision.
