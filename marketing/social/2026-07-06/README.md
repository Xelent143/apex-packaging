## LinkedIn Post

Rush packaging is rarely delayed by the plant first.

The schedule usually slips earlier:

1. Dimensions are incomplete
2. Artwork is not built to the dieline
3. Quantities change after quoting
4. Several people approve proofs in sequence
5. The structure is too complex for the actual deadline

If the project has to move fast, the first RFQ should include:

- finished dimensions
- packed weight
- quantity
- artwork status
- delivery location
- required in-hand date
- one clear approver

That gives the supplier room to recommend the fastest practical route, whether that is a simple corrugated carton, a printed mailer, or a design-support step before production.
