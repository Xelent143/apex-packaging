## LinkedIn Post

Poly tubing and poly sheeting solve a different problem than a pre-made bag.

They are usually the stronger buying decision when:

1. Product lengths vary and fixed bag inventory creates waste
2. Oversized items need custom cut coverage
3. The line already cuts and seals film at the workstation
4. Procurement wants fewer finished-bag SKUs on the shelf

The common mistake is asking for "clear poly" before defining the format.

A stronger RFQ starts with:

- tubing, flat sheeting, center-fold, or finished bag
- layflat width and cut length range
- gauge target or performance requirement
- seal method and loading style
- vent, perforation, food-contact, or PCR-content needs
- delivery location, volume, and release schedule

The newest Apex guide breaks down when roll stock beats a pre-made bag and which specifications help suppliers quote the right film faster.

## Facebook / Instagram Caption

Not every film request should start with a finished bag.

If the job involves variable lengths, oversized items, pallet covers, liners, or mixed-SKU packing, poly tubing or flat sheeting may be the better format.

Why buyers switch to roll stock:

- exact cut lengths at the point of use
- fewer finished-bag SKUs to manage
- better fit for pallet covers, liners, and dust barriers
- cleaner quotes when the line setup is defined early

Before sending an RFQ, include:

- the film format you need
- layflat width
- cut length range
- gauge target
- seal method
- vent or perforation needs
- manual or automated loading style
- volume and ship-to details
