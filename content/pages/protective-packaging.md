# Protective packaging — engineered to the protection level, not pulled off a shelf.

For Canadian shippers moving fragile, high-value, or rust-prone product. Apex specifies, manufactures, and supplies protective packaging that matches the product's weight, drop-test class, and shipping environment.

## Custom foam inserts

- **PE (polyethylene) foam** — closed-cell, water-resistant, good for general protection at moderate weight.
- **PU (polyurethane) foam** — softer, conforms to delicate surfaces (instruments, glassware).
- **ESD / anti-static foam** — for electronics, PCBs, sensitive components.
- **Cross-linked PE** — fine-cell foam for premium presentation (cases, instrument layouts).

Apex's design team converts product CAD or physical samples into a cavity-cut foam insert. Material grade and density are selected to product weight and drop-test requirement. Typical lead time: 7–10 business days from approved design.

## Cushioning systems

- **Instapak®** — foam-in-place urethane that expands around the product. Best for high-value, irregularly shaped, or one-off shipping.
- **Engineered corner pads** — pre-cut polyethylene corner blocks.
- **Suspension and retention packs** — for premium electronics and instruments.

## Rust prevention and corrosion protection

For metal parts shipped or stored over time:

- **VCI paper** — releases corrosion-inhibitor vapour in sealed packaging.
- **VCI film bags** — paired with desiccant for marine and humid environments.
- **Desiccants** — silica gel, clay, in various sizes.

Apex's metal-parts shipping kits combine VCI film, desiccant, foam corner protection, and the appropriate carton — pre-built or designed to spec.

## Drop-test and protection-class specification

For high-value shipments, Apex specifies the packaging stack against a drop-test class (ISTA 1A, 2A, 3A, 6-Amazon, etc.). The output is a documented spec — carton + foam + void fill — that meets the test class and is reproducible across all shipments.
