# Industries Apex packages for.

Custom packaging isn't generic. The right corrugated for automotive parts is different from the right corrugated for produce, and the right polybag for electronics is different from the right polybag for apparel. The Apex team has shipped product into each of the following verticals.

## Automotive parts manufacturers

Heavy single-wall and double-wall corrugated for engine components, body panels, and aftermarket accessories. Custom dieline inserts to immobilize parts. VCI rust-prevention for ferrous components. Wicketed polybags for high-speed bagging lines on small fasteners and trim. Typical buyer: a tier-2 supplier shipping daily into an OEM plant.

## Food and beverage producers

FDA-compliant corrugated and polybag film. SQF-certified facilities on relevant lines. Wicketed produce bags, pound bags, gusseted bread bags, wicketed bakery liners. Custom-printed retail cartons. Typical buyer: a regional food brand or co-packer with seasonal volume swings.

## Pharmaceuticals and medical devices

Validated corrugated for medical-device shipping. Sterile-barrier polybag film. Cold-chain insulating cartons (paired with PCM or gel inserts). Tamper-evident closures. Typical buyer: a Health Canada-licensed manufacturer with audit-ready packaging requirements.

## Electronics and high-tech

Anti-static (pink) polybags. ESD foam inserts and corner pads. VCI bagging for sensitive metals. Custom-cut foam for instruments, PCBs, and assembled units. Drop-test specified to ISTA 1A, 2A, or 3A. Typical buyer: an OEM or contract manufacturer with humidity and ESD requirements.

## Industrial supply and MRO distribution

Bulk corrugated, stretch and shrink film at pallet quantities, edge protectors, strapping. Scheduled releases against blanket POs. Single account manager. Typical buyer: an industrial distributor running multiple monthly truckloads.

## E-commerce and D2C brands

Custom-printed mailer boxes (250-unit MOQ), custom polymailers, tissue paper, branded tape, inserts. Full design support — logo to dieline to 3D mockup in 48–72 hours. Typical buyer: a Canadian D2C brand shipping 500–10,000 units a month.
