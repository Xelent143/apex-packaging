# Send a logo. Get a print-ready dieline and a 3D mockup in 48–72 hours.

Most Canadian custom-packaging buyers don't have a packaging-trained designer on staff. They have a logo, a product dimension, and a launch date. Apex's in-house design team converts that into a print-ready, manufacturable spec.

## What's included

1. **Structural design** — a dieline drawn to your product dimensions and the box style that fits (RSC, mailer, die-cut, custom). Includes flute selection, glue tab placement, score lines, and stacking guidance.
2. **Print prep** — colour conversion (RGB → CMYK or spot Pantone), bleed and trim allowances, file format ready for plate or digital production.
3. **3D mockup** — a rendered visualization of the finished box, viewable in browser, so the buyer can approve before any physical sample is produced.
4. **Physical pre-production sample** — optional, billed at sample cost, credited to the order on production.

## Turnaround

- **First dieline + 3D mockup**: 48–72 hours from receipt of brand assets.
- **Revisions**: 24–48 hours per round.
- **Physical sample (if requested)**: 5–7 business days after digital approval.

## What we need from you

- Logo as a vector file (AI, EPS, or PDF). If you only have raster (JPG, PNG), Apex's team will convert it — included.
- Brand colours as Pantone codes or hex values.
- Product dimensions (length × width × height) and weight.
- Quantity, target lead time, and any sustainability requirements.
- Reference images, if you have them — competitor packaging you like or dislike is useful.

## What it costs

- **Free with any order over MOQ.** If you place a production order, design is included up to two rounds of revision.
- **$200–$500 flat fee for design-only engagements**, depending on complexity. Common when buyers want a dieline before committing to a supplier.
- **Custom structural design** (entirely new dieline, no existing reference): quoted separately, typically $400–$1,200 depending on complexity.

## FAQ

**Can I keep the dieline file if I work with a different printer?**
For design-only paid engagements, yes — Apex provides the editable file. For design included with a production order, the dieline is licensed to the order; if the buyer wants portable rights, a one-time licensing fee applies.

**Do you do graphic design (artwork) too, or only structural?**
Both. Structural (dieline + 3D) is the default included scope. Surface artwork (illustration, layout) is available at additional cost — typically $400–$1,500 per panel.

**What file formats do you produce?**
PDF/X-4 for print production. AI / EPS native files for revisions. PNG / WEBP for 3D mockup previews.

**How does this differ from a packaging designer agency?**
Agencies focus on creative direction and brand. Apex's design team focuses on manufacturability — making sure the box actually folds, prints, and ships at the spec the agency drew. The two are complementary; many of Apex's clients have an agency for creative and Apex for production-ready files.
