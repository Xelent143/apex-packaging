# Strategy — Apex Packaging Solutions

## Positioning statement (the one sentence)

> **Apex Packaging Solutions is the Canadian custom-packaging manufacturer for industrial and B2B buyers who want corrugated boxes and polybags in 7–14 days at MOQs other suppliers won't touch — with in-house design help and FSC options built in, unlike legacy suppliers who quote 4–6 weeks and force 5,000-unit minimums.**

That's the spine of every page.

## Voice doc

### 3 do's

1. **Lead with numbers.** Days. Units. Percent recycled. ECT rating. Specific verticals. The buyer is a procurement person — vague claims insult their job.
2. **Talk to procurement, not marketing.** Plant managers, ops directors, buyers. They want lead times, MOQs, certifications, and a real human to call. They do not want "your packaging journey."
3. **Define jargon inline.** When you say RSC, follow with "(Regular Slotted Carton — the standard shipping box style)". When you say ECT-32, explain it once. Respect the reader's time and intelligence simultaneously.

### 3 don'ts

1. **Never say "partner", "trusted", "solutions" used as a generic noun, "premium", "world-class", "leading", "renowned".** These are the entire vocabulary of every Canadian packaging competitor. We do not sound like them.
2. **Never lead with "we" or "at Apex".** Lead with the buyer's situation or a specific number.
3. **Never claim something we don't quantify.** "Fast turnaround" is banned. "Production starts within 48 hours of artwork sign-off; boxes ship in 7–14 days" is allowed.

### 3 reference sentences (the gold standard)

1. "Most Canadian box suppliers quote 4–6 weeks. Apex prints, dies, and ships in 7–14 days from artwork sign-off — domestic production, no overseas freight, no excuses for missing your launch."
2. "MOQ on custom-printed corrugated mailers starts at 250 units. On stock RSC boxes, 100. If you're testing a SKU or running a quarterly promo, you don't have to commit to a pallet of 5,000."
3. "FSC-certified liner, 100% recycled options on most flutes, soy-based inks, and compostable polybag film. Buyers with sustainability targets get a paper trail that survives an audit."

Every page is checked against these three. Drift = rewrite.

## Page-by-page copy briefs

### `/` Home
H1 intent: "Custom packaging, made in Canada, shipped in 7–14 days."
Reader: a procurement manager who searched "custom corrugated boxes Canada" and is comparing 4 quotes.
Key facts to land: 7–14 day turnaround, 100-unit MOQs, FSC + recycled options, in-house design help, 6 service categories, industries served (auto, food, electronics, pharma, industrial supply, e-comm).
Primary CTA: Request a quote (links to /contact form).
Secondary CTA: Call / direct phone.

### `/services/corrugated-boxes`
H1 intent: "Custom corrugated boxes in Canada — RSC, FOL, die-cut, telescoping."
Reader: someone choosing a box style for a specific product.
Must answer: which style fits my product? what flutes/strengths are available? what's the MOQ? what's the price range? how do I get a sample?
Lead with the styles (RSC, HSC, FOL, Five Panel Wrap, die-cut, telescoping) with one-paragraph each, image, common use case.

### `/services/mailer-boxes`
H1 intent: "Custom mailer boxes for Canadian e-commerce brands."
Reader: D2C brand operator choosing a branded mailer.
Must answer: print options (full-color outside, custom inside print), structural options (tuck-top, magnetic close, ZIP-tear, dispenser), MOQ on print (250–500), file requirements, sample process.

### `/services/polybags`
H1 intent: "Custom polybags manufactured in Canada — LDPE, HDPE, anti-static, custom-print."
Reader: a buyer needing bags for shipping, garment, food, electronics, or industrial use.
Must answer: bag types (flat, gusseted, wicket, coreless, slider, ZIP), gauges (mil thicknesses), custom print (1-color up to full-process), FDA / food-safe options, anti-static specs, MOQ.

### `/services/industrial-bulk-packaging`
H1 intent: "Industrial and bulk packaging for Canadian manufacturers."
Reader: plant manager / procurement at a factory shipping pallet quantities.
Must answer: Gaylord boxes / bulk containers, custom pallet wrap, stretch film grades, shrink film, void fill at scale, edge protectors, palletizing equipment supply.

### `/services/protective-packaging`
H1 intent: "Custom foam, void fill, and protective packaging for fragile and high-value goods."
Reader: shipping fragile / high-value items (electronics, instruments, medical, fine goods).
Must answer: custom-cut foam inserts, Instapak®, bubble wrap, air pillows, paper void fill, VCI rust-prevention.

### `/industries`
H1 intent: "Industries Apex packages for in Canada."
Sections: Automotive · Food & Beverage · Pharma & Medical · Electronics · Industrial Supply · E-commerce / D2C.
Per vertical: one paragraph on the specific packaging challenge and what Apex provides.

### `/sustainability`
H1 intent: "Sustainable packaging options that survive an ESG audit."
Sections: FSC certification (what it means, which products carry it), recycled content (% by product), inks (soy-based), polybag alternatives (PCR content, compostable EN 13432, paper mailers), carbon-footprint disclosure on request.

### `/design-support`
H1 intent: "Send a logo. Get a print-ready dieline and a 3D mockup."
Reader: a brand owner / marketing lead with no in-house packaging designer.
Must answer: what's included (dieline + structural design + print prep + 3D mockup), turnaround (48–72h on first draft), file types accepted, revisions, cost (free with order over MOQ, charged for design-only work).

### `/about`
H1 intent: a sentence about the company. No "Welcome to Apex."
Sections: short origin (when, where, why), capabilities (machinery + plant + certifications + workforce), team (real names if possible), what Apex won't do (set boundaries: e.g., "we don't ship internationally" or "we don't do glass/metal" — if true).

### `/contact`
H1 intent: "Send your packaging spec. Get a quote within 24 hours."
Form fields: name, company, email, phone, project type (dropdown — RFQ / sample / design help / general), short message, file upload (specs/artwork).

### `/blog`
Starter posts:
1. **"ECT-32 vs ECT-44: what the rating actually tells you about your box."** Explains corrugated strength ratings, when to choose which, with weight examples.
2. **"RSC, FOL, or telescoping — picking the right box style for shipping fragile items."** Visual guide.
3. **"Poly bag thickness: when 1.5 mil is enough and when you need 4."** Practical guide for shipping operations.

Each post 800–1200 words, real diagrams or photos, internal links to relevant service pages.
