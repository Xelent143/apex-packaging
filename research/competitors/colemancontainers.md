**1\. A Styles**

Businesses need many shapes and styles of boxes for multiple types of [consumer goods packaging](https://www.colemancontainers.com/industries/consumer-product-packaging/), and we will cater to all your varied requirements. Let’s take a look at some common styles:

![Corrugated cardboard box](https://www.colemancontainers.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/RSC-1.jpg)

**Regular Slotted Carton (RSC)** are manufactured from one piece of corrugated board which is scored and slotted to form a body having flaps for closing on each of two opposite faces. The lengthwise flaps normally meet at the centre of the box when folded. This is a highly efficient design for many applications as there is little manufacturing waste. The RSC can be used for most products and is the most common box style. Their versatility makes them a preferred choice for automotive packaging, [food packaging supplies](https://www.colemancontainers.com/industries/food-packaging-supplies/), [beverage packaging](https://www.colemancontainers.com/industries/beverage-packaging-solutions/), [medical device packaging](https://www.colemancontainers.com/industries/medical-packaging-solutions/) and many other consumer goods packaging that need secure, durable packaging.

![Boxes shape](https://www.colemancontainers.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/HSC-1.jpg)

**Half-Slotted Carton (HSC)** is very similar to the RSC but without 1 set of flaps.

![Corrugated cardboard box](https://www.colemancontainers.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/FOL-1.jpg)

**Full Overlap Slotted Carton (FOL)**– All flaps are the same length, approximately the box’s width. When closed, the outer flaps come within 1″ of a complete overlap. This box style is designed to withstand heavy-duty use and harsh handling conditions. When placed with its bottom panel facing down, the overlapping flaps enhance cushioning, offering superior protection for the contents. When positioned on its side, the extra thickness increases structural integrity, ensuring enhanced stacking strength and durability.

**2\. A Size**

![Corrugated cardboard box](https://www.colemancontainers.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/BND_HowToMeasure-1.jpg)

There are three dimensions that define the size of corrugated packaging supplies, and we always define these using the inside measurements so your box will fit your products perfectly:

Length – to the closest 1/16″

Width – to the closest 1/16″

Depth (or height) – to the closest 1/16″

Dimensions are based on the opening of an assembled box. Once open find the longer of the sides – this is considered the “Length”. The shorter of the 2 sides is considered the “Width”. The side perpendicular to the length and width is the “Depth” of the box.

**3\. A Box Strength**

The strength of a corrugated custom mailer box starts with its material. A corrugated sheet consists of 2 major components – liner and medium. Liner is the flat paper that covers both sides of the sheet, and the medium is the fluted paper found between the liners. The flute, when glued to the linerboards with a starch-based adhesive, resists bending and pressure from all directions. When placed vertically on its ends, the flutes form vertical columns  capable of supporting considerable amounts of weight.

Flutes come in several heights, the most common being the  C flute which is used for RSC’s, and the B flute, which is used for die-cut boxes.

Edge Crush Test (ECT) – ECT measures how much resistance the box has to being crushed. The factors that calculate ECT are the liners (flat part of box wall construction), the medium (fluted material), and the type of flute used (C, B, A, E). Coleman Containers offers corrugated boxes in:

**4\. A Closure**

Most customers choose cardboard boxes that are sealed with a glue tab on the inside of the carton. We can also provide specialty closures such as stitching.

**5\. A Colour / Printing**

Corrugated box liner is normally kraft (brown). Oyster (white) liner can be also ordered in most flutes and strengths.

Oyster (white) liner is often used where graphic and colour quality is an important factor. Standard colours are available for the Next Day delivery whereas special inks can be ordered for 5-day delivery.

Boxes can be printed up to 3 colours using flexographic printing, the most commonly used process in box printing. The designs can be provided by you or our graphic designers can create custom graphics for you.

#### [How are corrugated boxes made?](https://www.colemancontainers.com/products/corrugated-packaging-boxes/\#collapse4)

Corrugated boxes are made by sandwiching a fluted (wavy) inner layer between two flat linerboards. The process starts with raw paper material, which is shaped into the fluted medium using heat and pressure. It’s then glued between the liners to form a strong, shock-absorbing board. Coleman Containers uses advanced manufacturing technology to produce precise, high-strength corrugated boxes for a wide range of industries.
