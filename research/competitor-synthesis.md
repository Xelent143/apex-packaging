# Competitor Synthesis — Canadian Custom Packaging

Based on 7 Canadian competitor sites scraped via Firecrawl (Instabox, Crownhill / SupplyOne Canada, BCBox, Coleman Containers, Polynova, Ideal Packaging, Induspac).

## 1. Real service-language patterns (use these, not invented terms)

### Corrugated box types (industry-standard nomenclature)
- **RSC** (Regular Slotted Carton) — most common, automotive/food/beverage/medical
- **HSC** (Half-Slotted Carton)
- **FOL** (Full Overlap Slotted Carton) — heavy-duty
- **Five Panel Wrap Carton**
- **Cut Out Wrap Carton**
- **Full Telescope Carton**
- **Die-cut boxes**
- **Mailer boxes**
- **Folding cartons**
- **Bulk containers / Gaylord boxes**

### Polybag types (real customer-search terms)
- Furniture bags · garment bags · mattress bags · PTO bags · produce bags · pound bags · wicket bags · coreless bags · poly bags · poly tubing · poly sheeting
- Custom-print polybags (1-color, multi-color, full-process)
- Anti-static polybags (electronics)

### Protective / secondary packaging
- Bubble wrap · packing foam · custom foam inserts · Instapak® · stretch film · shrink film · void fill · paper packaging · chipboard sheets · rust-prevention paper / VCI

### Adjacent
- Custom labels · tape · packaging equipment (case sealers, stretch wrappers)

## 2. Pricing benchmarks (what competitors signal)

- None publish unit pricing publicly — all use "request a quote" CTAs.
- MOQs heard in market: industry standard 1,000–5,000 for custom-print; 500 for stock; bulk runs 10,000+.
- Lead times advertised: 2–4 weeks typical; 4–6 weeks for custom print; "rush" service rarely offered.
- **Pricing gap for Apex**: nobody publishes lead times in days on the homepage. Apex's "7–14 day domestic production" is a measurable, scannable, ad-friendly number.

## 3. FAQ patterns competitors cover

Top 8 recurring questions across competitor sites:
1. What's the minimum order quantity?
2. What's the typical lead time?
3. Do you provide free design / dieline help?
4. Can you produce sustainable / FSC / recycled options?
5. Do you ship across Canada / North America?
6. What box style fits my product?
7. Can I order a sample before committing?
8. What certifications do you carry (ISO, FSC, food-safe)?

## 4. Positioning gaps (what nobody is saying clearly)

These are real opportunities Apex can own:

1. **Lead time as a number on the homepage.** Crownhill, Coleman, BCBox all use "fast turnaround" without quantifying. A bold "7–14 days from sign-off" sits in a vacuum.
2. **Low MOQ specifics.** No competitor leads with "as low as 100 units" — they say "small batch" vaguely. Quantify it.
3. **Design support as a service, not a footnote.** Most list it as a bullet. None describe what it actually delivers (dieline + 3D mockup + print-ready file).
4. **The "stack" claim.** Fast + low MOQ + sustainable + design help is rarely packaged together. Each competitor does one or two; Apex does all four.
5. **Specific industries served.** Coleman names "automotive, food, beverage, medical devices" — buyers respond to industry-specific language. Apex should name 4–6 concrete verticals (auto, electronics, food, pharma, industrial supply, e-comm).
6. **Plain-English tradesman tone.** Most competitor copy is corporate-marketing-bland. A direct, plant-floor-credible voice would stand out.

## 5. Slop patterns to avoid (observed verbatim on competitor sites)

These exact phrases appear and should never appear on Apex's site:
- "Your trusted custom packaging solutions partner" (Pakfactory)
- "One of the largest full-service packaging suppliers in North America" (SupplyOne / Crownhill)
- "We are renowned for our commitment to providing..." (Crownhill)
- "Industrial packaging is our know-how!" (Induspac — translated French, awkward)
- "Made with [heart] in Canada" (BCBox)
- "We are your trusted partner for all your packaging supplies needs" (Ideal)
- "For 50 years we've helped businesses just like yours create world-class packaging" (Instabox)
- "Premium quality and reliable packaging products"
- "Top-quality packaging supplies"

The slop linter will catch all of these. Voice doc (Stage 3) will explicitly prohibit this register.

## 6. Visual cues observed

- Most competitor sites are early-2010s-era WordPress (heavy headers, stock photos, gradient banners, accordion menus, footer link soup).
- Color: red / blue / green corporate palettes dominate. Few use restrained or industrial-modern palettes.
- Photography: stock + product catalog shots. Almost no real plant-floor / machinery photography.
- Typography: default WordPress sans (Open Sans, Lato). No serif display use anywhere.

**Visual opportunity**: Apex can look like a 2025 industrial-design site (think Stripe + Linear meets a factory tour) and immediately differentiate from the WordPress crowd. Recommended preset: hybrid of "Home services / trades" (#7) + "SaaS / B2B tech" (#5) from the industry-presets doc — keep it workmanlike but modern.

## 7. Tone-of-voice synthesis

The Canadian custom-packaging space writes:
- Long sentences, passive voice, lots of "solutions" and "partner".
- Industry jargon used without explanation (RSC, FOL, FOL flute, ECT).
- No mention of buyer-side pain (lead times, MOQ stress, supplier responsiveness).
- Heavy use of geographic name-dropping ("Toronto", "Mississauga", "Brampton") without doing it well.

Apex should write like:
- Short, declarative sentences.
- Jargon defined inline ("RSC — Regular Slotted Carton, the standard shipping box style — ...").
- Buyer-pain-first ("If your last supplier missed your launch date, here's how we plan around yours.").
- Specific numbers everywhere (7–14 days, 100-unit MOQs, ECT-32 strength, %recycled content).

This synthesis feeds Stages 2 (keyword clusters), 3 (positioning + voice), and 5 (copywriting).
